## 3. Process

**Root Touch-Up:** Color mixed to match existing color, applied to the root growth zone, processed, rinsed, and styled. Clean, precise, targeted.

**All Over Color:** Formula mixed for the guest's desired result and natural starting level. Applied root-to-ends (or just to roots for gray coverage), processed, rinsed, toned if needed.

**Balayage:** No foils. The colorist paints lightener or color directly onto sections of hair using a sweeping, freehand motion. Each section is different — variation is the point. The color is processed in the open air, not in foil heat, which creates a softer, more natural result. Requires a consultation to assess starting color, desired result, and time needed.

**Foilayage:** Combines the freehand balayage technique with foil processing for more lift. The colorist paints, then foils selected sections. Used when the guest needs more lightening than open-air processing can achieve.

**Color Correction:** Multi-step process. May involve color removal, bleach baths, neutralization of unwanted tones, toning, and conditioning. Almost always takes more than one appointment for significant corrections. Michelle Yrigolla is the designated specialist.

**Vivid / Fantasy / Block Color:** Hair must be pre-lightened to the appropriate level. The vivid color (bright reds, blues, pinks, purples, greens, multicolor blocking) is then applied over the lightened canvas. Silviya Warren and Zaida Delgado are the specialists.

## 7. Common Questions & Misunderstandings

**"How is balayage different from highlights?"**
"Traditional highlights use foils — every section gets wrapped, and the result is a more uniform lightness throughout. Balayage is freehand painting without foils, so each section is different and it mimics how the sun actually lightens hair. The grow-out is dramatically more natural — there's no obvious root line because the color starts soft and blends down. A lot of guests can go 12 to 16 weeks between balayage appointments."

**"I want platinum blonde — can I do that in one visit?"**
"Depends on where you're starting. If you have very dark hair, getting to platinum safely is usually a process — a series of lightening appointments spaced out so the hair stays healthy. Michelle is the expert for that journey. She'll tell you exactly what it'll take and won't rush it in a way that damages your hair."

**"My color looks brassy — what do I do?"**
"That's a toner situation. A toner neutralizes the brassy/orange/yellow tones and brings the color back to where it should be. It's a quick service — starts at $55. If it keeps happening, it might also be a shampoo issue — sulfates in regular shampoo strip color. Pureology is sulfate-free by design, which is why Hush uses it."

**"I had box dye — can they still do color?"**
"They see that all the time. Box dye does complicate things — it changes how the hair absorbs professional color — but Michelle and the team have worked with it plenty. The consultation will tell you what's possible and what to expect. Don't skip the consultation if you have box dye history."

**"How do I maintain my color at home?"**
"Sulfate-free shampoo is the single biggest thing you can do. Pureology is what Hush uses and recommends — it's sulfate-free, color-safe, and genuinely great. They'll talk to you about what to use when you come in."

"Applied correctly and removed properly, not significantly. The key is having them soaked off by a professional — not peeled. Hush's nail team knows the technique. The damage you hear about usually comes from improper removal."

**"How often do I need fills?"**
"Every 2 to 3 weeks, depending on how fast your nails grow. You'll see the gap at the base as the natural nail grows — that's your signal."

## 3. Process

**Lash Extensions:**
The guest lies comfortably on a treatment table, eyes closed. Allison isolates each natural lash and applies extensions one at a time (classic) or in small fans (volume/hybrid) using semi-permanent adhesive. The service is meditative — the room is quiet, music plays softly. Most guests fall asleep. A full set takes 1.5 to 2.5 hours depending on the style. Fills replace the lashes that have shed with the natural lash cycle.

**Lash Lift & Perm:**
A chemical solution is applied to the natural lashes while they're held against a curved silicone rod, creating a permanent upward curl. The process takes 45–60 minutes. No extensions involved — your natural lashes are lifted and set. Results last 6–8 weeks.

**Lash / Brow Tint:**
A vegetable-based dye is applied to the lashes or brows and left to develop for a few minutes. The result is darker, more defined lashes or brows that last 4–6 weeks.

## 7. Common Questions & Misunderstandings

**"Which style should I get?"**
"Honest guidance: if you're not sure, hybrid is the most beautiful middle ground. Classic is natural — like great mascara that doesn't smudge. Volume is full and dramatic. Hybrid gives you texture and dimension without going full-glam. Most guests who think they want classic end up wishing they'd done hybrid."

**"Will extensions damage my natural lashes?"**
"Applied correctly and matched to the natural lash's weight — which is exactly how Allison works — extensions should not damage natural lashes. The damage comes from extensions that are too heavy or too long for the natural lash to support, or from picking or pulling them. Allison takes lash health seriously and matches accordingly."

**"How long do they last?"**
"The extensions shed with your natural lash cycle — which turns over about every 6 to 8 weeks. Fills every 2 to 3 weeks keep them looking full. If you go longer without a fill, you'll notice patchiness as lashes shed."

**"I have completely straight lashes — is a lift worth it?"**
"Honestly, yes. Allison herself has straight lashes and never goes without her lift. She's said she 'would not leave the house without it.' A lash lift takes your natural lashes and makes them look wider-awake, mascara-ready, and open. It's genuinely transformative for straight-lash people."

**"Can I wear mascara with extensions?"**
"With extensions, you don't need to — that's the point. If you do, use a water-based formula only, never oil-based. With a lash lift, you absolutely can wear mascara — and most guests find it looks even better because the lashes are actually facing the right direction."

## 3. Process

**Signature Facial:**
Begins with a skin analysis — the esthetician assesses skin type, condition, and current concerns. Deep cleanse, exfoliation, steam to open pores, extractions if appropriate, custom mask, serum, and moisturizer. Each facial is genuinely customized — not a scripted, one-size-fits-all protocol.

**Dermaplaning:**
Sterile blade held at a 45-degree angle, gently scraping the skin surface to remove dead skin cells and vellus hair (the fine "peach fuzz"). It looks intimidating but feels like a gentle, light scrape. Leaves skin silky smooth.

**HydraFacial:**
Four-step technology-driven treatment: (1) lymphatic drainage and detox; (2) deep cleanse and chemical peel; (3) vacuum-powered painless extractions; (4) saturation with antioxidants and hyaluronic acid. Immediate, visible glow. Zero downtime.

**Microdermabrasion:**
Diamond-tip wand resurfaces the skin through mechanical exfoliation. Reduces fine lines, uneven texture, and dullness. More aggressive than a standard facial exfoliation, less intensive than microneedling.

**Microneedling:**
Numbing cream applied 30–45 minutes before the service. The esthetician then uses a microneedling device to create tiny, controlled micro-channels in the skin. The body's healing response stimulates collagen and elastin production. Afterward, skin is pink and flushed — similar to a mild sunburn — for 24–48 hours. A series of 3–6 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart produces optimal results.

**Airbrush Spray Tan:**
Custom airbrush application — the technician adjusts shade and technique for your skin tone. The result is a natural-looking, streak-free tan.
